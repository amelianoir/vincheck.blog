# VIN Check for Electric Vehicles: What EV Buyers Need to Know

Electric vehicles have unique considerations. Here's what to check before buying a used EV.

## Why EVs Need Special Attention

Unlike gas cars, EVs have:
- **Battery degradation** (expensive to replace)
- **Software issues** (recalls and updates)
- **Charging system problems**
- **High-voltage component damage**

## What to Look for in an EV VIN Report

### 1. 🔋 Battery Health History
Check for:
- Battery replacement records
- Degradation complaints
- Warranty claims
- Thermal management issues

### 2. ⚡ Charging System Damage
Look for:
- Supercharger/fast charging abuse
- Charging port damage
- Electrical system repairs

### 3. 🔧 Recall History
EVs have frequent recalls for:
- Battery fires
- Software bugs
- Charging issues
- Autopilot problems

### 4. 💥 Accident Damage
**Critical for EVs:** High-voltage battery damage can be catastrophic.

A VIN check reveals:
- Collision history
- Structural damage
- Battery compartment damage

## Tesla-Specific Checks

### Autopilot Hardware
- Which version is installed?
- Has it been upgraded?
- Any accident history with Autopilot engaged?

### Supercharger Access
- Is free supercharging included?
- Has it been transferred?

### Software Version
- Is it up to date?
- Any known bugs?

## Red Flags for Used EVs

🚩 **Low price** for high mileage (battery degradation)
🚩 **No service records** (hiding problems)
🚩 **Salvage title** (battery damage risk)
🚩 **Multiple owners** in short time (lemon)
